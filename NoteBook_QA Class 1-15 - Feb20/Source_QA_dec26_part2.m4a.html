Guide:
The source is a pedagogical lecture focused on the principles of prime factorization and its practical application in determining divisibility rules for composite numbers. The instructor explains that while general factors for a number like 24 can vary, its prime factorization provides a unique identification that remains consistent regardless of the method used to derive it. This concept is then extended to testing divisibility; to check if a large number is divisible by a composite divisor, one must break that divisor into its prime components and verify that the number is divisible by each prime raised to its highest necessary power. The text further introduces a systematic technique for finding the highest power of a prime contained within a factorial, such as calculating how many times the number five fits into 100!. This is achieved through successive division, where the quotient of each step is divided again by the prime until no further division is possible. Finally, the lecture addresses composite powers in factorials, teaching students to identify the limiting prime factor—the prime that appears least frequently—to determine how many times a composite number like 10 or 12 can perfectly divide the larger factorial expression.
