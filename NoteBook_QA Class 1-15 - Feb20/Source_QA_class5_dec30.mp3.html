Guide:
This lecture serves as a comprehensive guide to solving advanced number theory problems, specifically focusing on factorials and digit patterns. The instructor first details the methodology for finding the highest power of a prime number within a factorial through successive division, later extending this logic to composite numbers by analyzing their constituent prime factors. A practical application of this is demonstrated in calculating the number of trailing zeros in large factorials, which essentially involves determining the maximum power of ten by counting the availability of the prime factor five. The second half of the session shifts toward last digit and last two-digit calculations, emphasizing the concept of cyclicity. By identifying that the units digit of any power follows a repeating pattern (usually in cycles of four), students are taught to use modular arithmetic to simplify complex exponents. For more intricate "last two-digit" problems, the tutor introduces binomial expansion as an elegant tool to break down bases into manageable forms, such as $70-1$. Ultimately, the lesson bridges the gap between theoretical number properties and the efficient mental shortcuts required for competitive examinations.
